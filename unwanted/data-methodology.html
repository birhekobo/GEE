Data Sources and Methodology

Our gallery features imagery from a variety of leading Earth observation satellites and sensors. Understanding the source and processing of this data is key to its interpretation.

Satellite Systems & Sensors:

Landsat Program (NASA/USGS):

Provides decades of continuous Earth observation, crucial for long-term change detection. Sensors include TM, ETM+, and OLI/TIRS.

Learn more about Landsat

Sentinel Missions (ESA Copernicus):

A suite of satellites offering high-resolution radar and optical imagery. Key missions include Sentinel-1 (Radar) and Sentinel-2 (Optical).

Learn more about Sentinel Missions

MODIS (NASA Terra/Aqua):

Offers daily global coverage, ideal for monitoring large-scale phenomena like vegetation patterns, sea surface temperature, and cloud cover. Also provides key data products for atmospheric composition and aerosols.

Learn more about MODIS | Explore MODIS Aerosol Data

High-Resolution Commercial Satellites:

(e.g., WorldView, Pleiades, GeoEye) - Provide very detailed imagery for specific site analysis and mapping. Several commercial providers exist.

Explore Maxar | Explore Planet

CALIPSO (Cloud-Aerosol Lidar and Infrared Pathfinder Satellite Observation):

Provides vertical profiles of clouds and aerosols using lidar technology, crucial for understanding atmospheric composition and its impact on climate and air quality.

Learn more about CALIPSO

MISR (Multi-angle Imaging SpectroRadiometer):

Observes Earth from multiple angles to characterize aerosol properties, cloud types, and surface features, important for air quality and climate studies.

Learn more about MISR

Giovanni (NASA Data Portal):

A data portal that provides access to Earth observation data for analysis and visualization, simplifying the process of exploring and using satellite data.

Explore Giovanni

Earthdata Search:

NASA's primary portal for searching and discovering Earth science data from various missions and disciplines.

Explore Earthdata Search

TROPOMI (Sentinel-5P):

The TROPOspheric Monitoring Instrument (TROPOMI) aboard Sentinel-5P provides high-resolution measurements of atmospheric trace gases, including ozone, nitrogen dioxide, sulfur dioxide, methane, and carbon monoxide, supporting air quality and climate research.

Learn more about TROPOMI

GRACE (Gravity Recovery and Climate Experiment):

GRACE satellites measure changes in Earth's gravity field, enabling the monitoring of groundwater, ice sheets, sea level, and mass changes in the Earth system, providing critical data for hydrology and climate studies.

Learn more about GRACE

SMAP (Soil Moisture Active Passive):

Provides global measurements of soil moisture and freeze/thaw state, supporting weather forecasting, drought monitoring, and climate research.

Learn more about SMAP

Suomi NPP (National Polar-orbiting Partnership):

Collects data on atmospheric, land, and oceanic conditions, serving as a bridge between NASA's Earth Observing System satellites and the next-generation JPSS satellites.

Learn more about Suomi NPP

GOES (Geostationary Operational Environmental Satellites):

Provides real-time weather monitoring, severe storm tracking, and environmental observations for the Americas and surrounding oceans.

Learn more about GOES

Aqua (NASA):

Focuses on the Earth's water cycle, collecting data on precipitation, evaporation, and the cycling of water.

Learn more about Aqua

Terra (NASA):

Monitors Earth's land, atmosphere, and oceans, providing data for climate and environmental research.

Learn more about Terra

ERA5 (ECMWF Reanalysis v5):

ERA5 provides hourly estimates of a large number of atmospheric, land, and oceanic climate variables, using a combination of model data and observations, and is widely used for climate and weather research.

Learn more about ERA5

MERRA-2 (Modern-Era Retrospective analysis for Research and Applications, Version 2):

MERRA-2 is a NASA atmospheric reanalysis that assimilates a wide range of satellite observations, providing global data for weather, climate, and atmospheric composition studies.

Learn more about MERRA-2

CMIP6 (Coupled Model Intercomparison Project Phase 6):

CMIP6 is a collaborative framework designed to improve knowledge of climate change, providing multi-model climate projections used in IPCC reports and climate impact studies.

Learn more about CMIP6

Common Methodologies:

Radiometric Correction:

Adjusting for sensor irregularities and atmospheric effects to ensure accurate surface reflectance values.

Geometric Correction:

Orthorectification to remove distortions and align images with precise geographic coordinates.

Image Enhancement:

Techniques like histogram stretching or filtering to improve visual interpretability.

Classification:

(e.g., Supervised, Unsupervised) - Categorizing pixels into land cover classes (forest, water, urban, etc.).

Change Detection:

Analyzing multi-temporal imagery to identify and quantify changes on the Earth's surface over time.

Specific details regarding the data source and processing for each image can be found in its description where available.
